Educational Attainment in the Twenty-First Century

Examining trends over 20 years

By Alex Caban-Echevarria Published: August 16, 2023

Before the affirmative action ruling by the Supreme Court, college degree came to the forefront of discussion about hiring practices and diversity in the workplace, questioning whether the benchmark of a Bachelor’s degree in the United States was hurting or helping entry-level workers.

For many, going to college at 18 years old is a right of passage. For some, a Bachelor’s degree has become the norm that “degree inflation” has barred them from pursuing a career they may already have the skills for.

The current educational attainment trends show that 53,045,000 people in the US have their Bachelor’s degree, while 85,218,000 people have their Bachelor’s degree or above, which includes Master’s, professional, and Doctorate degrees. How do these statistics compare to ten or twenty years ago?
